Customizability


Mechanical keyboards are made up of a bunch of different parts, such as the case, switches, keycaps, etc. With so many different parts that go into putting a keyboard together, that means it has a lot of potential to be customized. Every part of a keyboard can be changed to fit how you want it to look, sound, and feel.

Here are a few of the basic customizable things:

Layouts

The main layouts are:

  • 60%
  • 65%
  • 75%
  • TKL
  • 100%/Full-sized

The different layouts tell you which keys/how many keys are included on the keyboard. For example, 60% keyboard means that it only includes 60% of a regular keyboard, so it excludes things like the number row, F-row, numpad, and arrow keys. But a 75% does include the number row and F-row and arrow keys, but still excludes a few extra keys and the numpad. Overall, the keyboard layout will determine the the functionality and size of the keyboard.

Switches

There are 3 different types of switches:

Type Feel Sound
Linear Smooth and consistent throughout the keystroke Only makes a sound when you push the key all the way down
Tactile A small "bump" when pressing down on the key Only makes a sound when you push the key all the way down
Clicky A small "bump" when pressing down on the key Makes a click noise when pressing past the "bump"

The different parts of a switch (the spring, top housing, bottom housing, and stem) also vary. The spring can come in different lengths and weights, which impact the feel of the switch. And the top housing, bottom housing, and stem can be made from different plastics that impact the sound of the switch. Overall, switches will change how typing feels and sounds.

Keycaps

The main profiles of keycaps are:

  • Cherry
  • OEM
  • SA
  • DSA
  • XDA

The different profiles of keycaps impact the feel of typing. Keycaps also come in two different plastics: ABS or PBT. They differ in feel and also practical things like durability. Overall, keycaps will change typing feels and also they heavily impact the aesthetic of the keyboard.
